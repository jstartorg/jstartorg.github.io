Many departments at Jumpstart use Cases in Salesforce to manage support requests. Some benefits of using Cases over a shared Outlook inbox are that they:

  • Can be assigned directly to a user and transferred between users
  • Link to other Salesforce records
  • Have data fields that allow us to track and report on the type of support requests received, who they're requested by, status, etc.
  • Contain the entire email thread history, and can even have multiple email threads linked
  • Give users the ability to add Tags to keep track of common themes, and @mention other users in Chatter posts to collaborate

Guidelines and Norms

Each department may have their own expectations about timeline, ways of responding, etc. If you're unsure, it's helpful to follow these guidelines (with the understanding that they cannot always be met due to capacity constraints, waiting on other people or external services, etc.):

  • Check your My Open Cases list daily
  • Try to acknowledge each request (even if simply to say "I've seen this and I will look into a resolution") ideally within one business week, but never later than one month.
  • Prioritize based on severity level and number of people impacted. I.e.:
    • Severity Levels
      • Requester/others can't complete many/any of their normal tasks: acknowledge, start working, and try to find a resolution immediately/within one business day.
      • Requester/others need this in order to complete an important task: acknowledge and start working within 3 business days, try to find a resolution or workaround within one business week (maybe faster if a lot of people are impacted).
      • This would help requester/others complete an important task, but they have a workaround: acknowledge within one business week, start working whenever capacity allows, try to resolve within one month (maybe faster if a lot of people are impacted), or inform requester it cannot be prioritized at this time.
      • This would be nice to have, but it's not important: acknowledge within one month, start working if/when capacity allows and it would benefit multiple people, or inform requester it cannot be prioritized at this time.

General Principles

  • Not only can you close out spam emails immediately, but senders can be added to the Microsoft 365/Exchange email domain block list and/or the Areas of Concern/Salesforce Custom Metadata record Jumpstart Settings field "Blocked Domains"
  • Similarly, there are many messages that are generated by system jobs but are not something to make a ticket for, so you can use the "Blocked Subject Lines" to add text that it will run a partial match on to skip. BE CAREFUL about the partial matches that they won't overreach ("the" is not a good partial match for instance)
    • Both settings are used in the On Save: Case Salesforce Flow to filter out incoming tickets
    • The assignment of Cases is handled by the Assignment Rules in Salesforce
  • Look out for requests that should actually go to other departments - even if there is a part of the request OpsTech can support, the priority should be to get work over to other teams as fast as possible
  • Clarify stewardship - the first place for any ticket to go should be to the person in charge of the originating system. If it is a Power BI error, straight to the Data Reporting Specialist, if an Active Directory error, the System Administrator; they can always identify which part of the ticket needs further support from another team member and reassign
  • Make your own List Views; when your list of Cases starts to get greater than 20 or whatever you feel like you can understand in a glance, make a new List View to group the tickets more effectively. Maybe that means by system, or by requesting department, whatever it takes to not feel like you are overwhelmed
  • Close out aggressively - if you send a response to someone that you feel should resolve the issue, don't be afraid to mark it as complete and not think about it anymore. If there are ongoing issues the person's response will reopen the ticket anyways
  • Use the priority settings to make clear distinctions quickly before trying to resolve any modestly complicated request

Background Information

The following addresses (and their aliases) are forwarded into Salesforce as Cases. Encourage requesters to only include one of these addresses in their request, as including multiple will create duplicate Cases (see Duplicate Management below). Also discourage requesters from including individual support team members' addresses, so that things don't get out of sync. If your department is not yet using Cases and would like to, email salesforce@jstart.org and ask your Salesforce Administrator to set up Email-to-Case for you.

  • support@jstart.org
  • salesforce@jstart.org
  • myjstart@jstart.org
  • jstats@jstart.org
  • evaluation@jstart.org
  • fiscal.support@jstart.org
  • per@jstart.org
  • program.support@jstart.org

When a Case is created from one of these emails, it will be automatically assigned to the default owner for that address (contact salesforce@jstart.org to get your department's Case Assignment Rules updated). Cases can be owned by a user or a queue. Each department triages their Cases a little differently, so refer to your department's guidance on that.

Each address also has an auto-response set up (contact salesforce@jstart.org to update your Case Auto-Response), which contains details from the Case, most importantly the thread id. Salesforce will automatically link any email with the thread id in the subject or the body to that Case. The auto-response encourages requesters to reply to the auto-response directly with any additional questions or updates so that it is linked to their original request. If they don't, Salesforce may still find the correct Case to link it to, but it may create a duplicate (see Duplicate Management below).

Automations

  • Certain classes of error are misleading and just indicate an incomplete automation that is still accomplishing its core responsibility - identify those and put them On-Hold
  • Similarly, automation update messages can be treated as lower priority - some systems are more chatty than others, identify and remove them quickly
  • Automation messages that refer to system connections themselves should be treated as high priority as they often affect multiple actual automations

Working on a Case

Updating Case Data

Case work is primarily done from the My Open Cases list view. Some users will have this list view on their home page, others will have to click on the Cases tab in the navigation bar (if you don't have the tab, watch this video to learn how to add it) and switch the view to My Open Cases (if not already pinned). This shows all Cases assigned to the logged in user where the Status is not Waiting on Response, Waiting on MSP, On Hold, or one of the closed statuses. You can sort the list by any column by clicking on the column header. Click it again to change the sort from ascending to descending.

  • Click on the Case Number or Subject to view the details of a Case.
  • View/Update the following fields
    • Status
      • On Hold: Use this if there is something blocking progress on the Case but you don't want to close it yet. This will move it out of your My Open Cases list view, so be sure to check your My On Hold Cases list view from time to time.
      • New: Default status, no one has assessed the Case yet.
      • Intake: Use this once the Case is assigned to the correct person and has all the detail needed for the owner to start working on it.
      • Waiting on Requester: Status will automatically change to this when you send an email from the Case. This will move it out of your My Open Cases list view, so be sure to check your My Waiting On Response Cases list view from time to time to either nudge the requester or close it out.
      • In Progress: Use this to indicate the Case is currently being worked on.
      • Responded: Status will automatically change to this when an incoming email is received after you've contacted the requester.
      • Escalated: Use this to either flag for yourself that the request has become more urgent, or if you need urgent support from someone else, transfer it to them with this status.
      • Reopened: Status wil automatically change to this when an incoming email is received after the Case has already been closed.
      • Closed: You can choose between 3 different closed statuses:
        • Complete: All work is complete and all notes/details documented.
        • Not Required: No action needs to be taken on this Case (requester figured it out for themselves, a validation error that doesn't need to be resolved, etc.).
        • Not at this time: Maybe the Case would ideally be completed but there is no capacity, or it cannot be prioritized.
    • Type: Select the most appropriate.
    • Requesting Department: Select the most appropriate.
    • Contact: Should auto-populate with the requester's Contact record, if not you can add it.
    • Lead: If the Case is related to a Corps Member, add their Lead record here.
    • Organization: If the Case is related to an organization (site, funder, etc.) add the Account record here.
    • Priority: Defaults to Medium usually, but you can switch it to Low or High if tracking things by priority.
    • Target Date: (Optional) Use this to track when you would like to complete the request by.
  • Tags: In the Tags section on the right-hand side, you can type in words and phrases that the Case is related to. This gives you a flexible way to track common themes among Cases that don't fit within the data fields above (i.e. corpsmemberapplication, login, readfortherecord, JStats, etc.). Once you start typing, existing Tags that start with those same letters will appear in the dropdown, and you can either select one or create a new one. If you click on a Tag from a Case, in the Related tab you can see other Cases with that Tag.
  • You can also make the same update to multiple records in bulk from the list view:
    • Check off the records you want to edit.
    • Click the Case Details button.
    • Make your changes in the window that pops up.
    • Click Save.

Responding to and Closing a Case

Once all the Case data has been updated and you have all the information you need to respond:

  • Click the Email button in the top right.
    • From: Should default to the support address that was sent to. If it hasn't, select it. Sometimes it may default to your individual address. If that happens, always change it to a support address, otherwise when the requester responds it will go straight to you rather than Salesforce.
    • To: This will often default to the Contact record/email address of the requester. If not, you can either search and select the Contact record (it will send to the Contact's Preferred Email), or type in the email address. You can add multiple recipients.
    • Cc: Select or type any recipients you would like to cc.
    • Bcc: Select or type any recipients you would like to bcc.
      • By default, your Email Settings will be set to bcc your individual address. To turn this off, go to your Personal Settings and edit the Automatic Bcc option in your Email Settings.
    • Type your response. The email composer includes many of the formatting options you'd find in a regular email service.
      • Templates: For frequently asked questions, you can use Email Templates so you're not typing the same response over and over. To insert a template, click the insert, create, or update template icon in the bottom left of the email composer and select the template you want to insert. You can still modify the email before sending. (need to add instructions for creating templates).
      • Attachments: Click the paperclip icon in the bottom left of the email composer to attach a file. Files either you or the requester attach to emails on the Case will be saved to the Files on the right-hand side of the record.
      • Email Signature: You can update your email signature in the Email Settings referenced above.
      • Send the email.
    • The Status will automatically update to Waiting on Requester and the record will be removed from your My Open Cases list view and added to your My Waiting on Response Cases list view.
    • Use Chatter Posts to make any notes that aren't already a part of your emails with the requester (i.e. progress notes, steps you took to resolve it, people you're waiting on information from, links to external resources, etc.). This allows other team members to see where you're at and helps future you/others remember what to do if this issue comes up again.
      • Click the Post button in the top right.
      • Type your notes.
      • Click Share.
      • Post will be added to the Case Feed.
    • When the requester responds again, the Status will automatically update to Responded.
    • If you are already working on a request and you receive a separate email about it to your individual address, you can link it to the Case by forwarding it to the support address and including the thread id (which you can find if you click the Email button on the Case and scroll all the way to the bottom of the email composer).
    • Go back and forth as many times as needed until you've reached a resolution, provided a workaround, or informed the requester why you can't provide a resolution at this time.
    • Close the Case
      • Click Closed and then Select Closed Status in the path at the top of the page.
      • Choose the appropriate Status.
      • Click Save.

Collaborating with Other Support Agents

  • Transfer Ownership: If you need another support agent to take action on the Case, you can transfer it to them in either of the two ways below. If you have responded to the requester before transferring it to someone else, make sure to change the Status back to something other than Waiting On Requester (such as In Progress) so that it shows in their My Open Cases list view. They can either close the Case themselves or transfer it back to you.
    • Simply update the Owner field on the record.
    • Use the Change Owner button in the top right. This also gives you the option to send them an email notifying them that the Case has been transferred to them (which is useful for folx who may not look at their Cases daily).
  • Chatter: You can @mention other users in Chatter Posts to have a conversation about the Case. Users will receive a notification in Salesforce (and most also via email, though users have the ability to turn that off) when they are mentioned in a post, or when someone replies to a post they made.

Duplicate Management

If the requester emails multiple support addresses, or sends in a separate email instead of replying to the auto-response, and a duplicate Case is created, you can merge it with the original:

  • Click the Merge This Case button in the top right.
  • Search for the primary Case by either the Case Number or Subject and click Next.
  • Select the primary Case and click Next.
  • Confirm you have selected the appropriate primary and merged Cases and click Next.
  • You can choose to either go to the primary Case or go to your Home page.
  • A Chatter post will be added to the Case Feed of the primary Case with details from the merged Case.
  • The merged Case will be marked as Not Required and the primary Case will be added to the Parent Case field on the merged Case.

Files and Chatter comments will not be automatically moved from the merged Case to the Primary Case, so it might be a good idea to do so yourself so that they're not lost.
