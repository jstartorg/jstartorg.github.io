No Action Yet

When a prospective Corps Member first submits an application at my.jstart.org/apply, a Volunteer Registration record (and a Person record if they don't already have one) will be automatically created using the details they entered on the application. The Registration Stage will be Applying, and the Application Process Stage will be No Action Yet.

This is the default Application Process Stage. Follow the steps below to move to the next stage.

  • Receive new application notification in email/see new applications in New Applications list view on Salesforce homepage

  • Go to Corps Member's Person record in Salesforce to view characteristics like contact information, birthday, permanent address, etc.

    1. Highlights panel (top): most important information about the Corps Member

    2. Information panel (bottom left):

      a. Relevant Details tab: lists the Corps Member's service history (Volunteer Registrations), Organization Affiliations, and interest in future Jumpstart opportunities (Campaign History).

      b. Complete Details tab: all contact information and other details about the Corps Member

      c. Audit Trail tab: history of changes for certain tracked fields

    3. Actions panel (bottom right): under development

  • Go to Corps Member's Volunteer Registration record in Salesforce to view application and service details like member type, term address, service site, availability, federal work study information, etc. (If they have applied to the wrong site, please email salesforce@jstart.org to move them to the correct one).

    1. Highlights panel (top): registration stage, key details related to that stage

    2. Information panel (bottom left):

      a. Relevant Details tab: most important information about the Corps Member for that program year

      b. Complete Registration tab: all information about the Corps Member for that program year - only go here if you need info that's not in the other sections, as it's not as focused as the others

      c. Audit Trail tab: history of changes for certain tracked fields

      d. Secure Documents tab: pdf files of application & attachments, CHC documents, stipend forms, exit forms, etc. get saved here

    3. Actions panel (bottom right): buttons and tools you can use to move the Corps Member along the enrollment process, send emails, send forms, etc.

Scheduling and Interviewing

In the Volunteer Registration record:

  • Schedule Interview
    • Optional: Use Send Interview Invitation action in the actions panel to send the applicant potential interview times, and include a customized message in the interview invite. This will send them a Calendly link where they can either accept one of the interview times or request a different time (the email explains that if they request a different time, site staff will need to confirm if that time works or not). When they select a time, everyone who is listed as site staff in Salesforce will receive an email (at your jstart address) with a calendar attachment that you can use to create an event on your calendar.
      • This automatically moves the Application Process Stage to Scheduling
      • If applicant accepts one of the interview times, Interview: Scheduled Date will automatically fill in (you can manually change this if the interview is rescheduled)
    • If you don't use the above tool, you can just manually coordinate with the applicant and fill in the Interview: Scheduled Date yourself
    • Once Interview: Scheduled Date is filled in, Application Process Stage will move to Interviewing

Offering

In the Volunteer Registration record:

Before sending the member any forms, be sure to fill in the following information:

  • Service Start Date and Service Exit Date: these dates can be updated at any time and don't need to be exact yet, but are needed for the following:
    • The offer letter will include the months of the dates you enter here.
    • If the member has selected the wrong program year, entering these dates will correct it.
    • These dates are also necessary to calculate exemption eligibility.
  • For AmeriCorps members, set the National and Community Service Type to AmeriCorps. This will ensure that the correct actions are available to you on their record, and that the member receives the correct electronic forms.
  • If the member has selected the wrong program season (i.e. School Year when it should have been Summer), you can correct that in the Program Model field.

In the actions panel, use the following buttons to communicate with the applicant and send them an offer letter and other forms needed to begin enrollment. See here for more about the information needed to make these buttons available, and to move the record to the next stage.

  • Send Authorization Form: This will send the member the Jumpstart authorization form, prefilled with their name, social security number, phone number, service site, service state, and residence state (term address). When the member signs and submits, the History Check: Authorization Form Signed date will be automatically updated. If the form is submitted through other means, you can fill in the History Check: Authorization Form Signed date manually.
    • If the member is eligible for exemption, you don't need to send an authorization form. Just enter the Verified Exemption date (see below for more details).
  • Send Offer Form: This will email the Corps Member the offer letter, prefilled with their name, member type, and service months and years, and automatically move the Application Process Stage to Offering. When the Corps Member signs and submits, the Accepted Offer Date will be automatically updated. If the form is submitted through other means, or if your site does not require the offer form and you do not wish to use it, you can fill in the Accepted Offer Date manually.
  • When Accepted Offer Date (and History Check: Authorization Form Signed if member is not exempt) is filled in, this will automatically move Application Process Stage to Accepted Offer and Registration Stage to Enrolling.

Close Application

If you choose not to go forward with this applicant or they cannot serve with Jumpstart:

  • Click the Close Application button in the actions panel and fill out the following information:

    • Select the Closed Type, or how/why the close was initiated.
    • Select the Closed Reason to provide additional details on why the application is being closed. The options for this depend on the Closed Type selected. Some Closed Types don't have any associated Closed Reasons, in which case you can just leave this blank.
    • Add any comments you have as needed.
    • Indicate whether to consider the member for next year.
  • Once you click Next, you'll be taken to a screen where you can confirm you are ready to completely close the application, as you will no longer be able to edit the record once you do so. Confirming and clicking Next will automatically move the Application Process Stage to Closed Application and Registration Stage to Not Hired. The Closed Registration Details section on the Volunteer Registration will show all the information you just entered, including the Registration Stage at the time of closing.

Reopen Application

If you mark someone as Not Hired and then it later turns out that the applicant is able to continue with the enrollment process, you can reopen the application by clicking the Reopen Application button in the actions panel. This will clear the data you entered when you closed the application (Closed Type, Closed Reason, Closed Reason Notes, Closed in Stage, Consider for Next Year), and move the Registration Stage back to Applying and the Application Process Stage back to where it was before you closed it.
