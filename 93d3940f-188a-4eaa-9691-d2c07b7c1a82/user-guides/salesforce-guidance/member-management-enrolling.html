Enrolling

During this stage in the process, the highlights panel on the Volunteer Registration will show the member's criminal history check Status alongside a list of CHC requirements and key dates. You can either enter all required CHC data or verify exemption (see Exemption Verification below) in these sections.

Before conducting any checks or sending the member any forms, be sure to:

  • Ensure the Service Start Date is accurate. The Service Exit Date should also be as close to accurate as possible, but can be updated later.
  • Ensure the Member Type is accurate. This is needed to prefill the enrollment packet correctly.
  • Select the appropriate Benefits Type. This is needed to prefill the enrollment packet correctly.
  • Ensure the Supervisor is accurate. This is needed to prefill the enrollment packet correctly and ensure the right person receives the notification when the packet is signed by the member. (This is auto-filled by the Default Corps Member Supervisor on the Organization Agreement. The default can be changed on the Organization Agreement to ensure any newly created Volunteer Registrations get the new default).
    • If the Benefits Type = Stipend, further steps will need to be taken to set up the member in Paycom. Please see Finance's instructions on Corps Member stipends in Salesforce and Paycom for more details.
  • If the person will be an AmeriCorps member, set the National and Community Service Type to AmeriCorps. This will ensure that the correct actions are available to you on their record, and that the member receives the correct electronic forms.

Criminal History Checks

On individual Volunteer Registration records, enter all your CHC information in the highlights panel pictured above. (If you need to view all the dates you've entered or update one that was entered incorrectly, you can do so in the Complete Registration tab. However, do not use the Complete Registration tab for regular CHC data entry, because it cannot mark non-required checks as N/A the way the tool above can).

  • If a certain check is not required for that member, or is covered by another check, it will say N/A, and you won't need to enter any data there.
  • If the check is required but the member can begin service before it is cleared, there will be space for you to enter the Initiation Date. Then, click the Save icon to the right.
  • After saving the Initiation Date (or if clearance is required to serve and initiation dates aren't needed), you can enter the Clearance Date in the same spot and click Save. The saved Clearance Date will then display in green.
  • Once all required criminal history check dates have been entered, this will automatically move the criminal history check Status to Cleared for Training and Service.

You can also enter Initiation and Clearance Dates in bulk by going to the Criminal History Checks tab of the current member and child information panel on your home page.

Finish Enrollment

In addition to entering CHC data or exemption verification, the Contract Signed Date must be filled in. This will happen automatically when the member signs the electronic enrollment packet (see Helpful Tools below), or you can fill it in manually if the form is signed through other means.

Once the CHC Status = Cleared for Training and Service and the Contract Signed Date is filled in, this will automatically move the Registration Stage to Serving.

Close Enrollment

If the person will no longer continue with the enrollment process:

  • If you're storing member files electronically, complete and upload any relevant forms to the member's Secure Documents. See the Helpful Tools section below for the electronic forms available during this stage.
  • Click the Close Enrollment button in the actions panel and fill out the following information:

    • Select the Closed Type, or how/why the close was initiated.
    • Select the Closed Reason to provide additional details on why the enrollment is being closed. The options for this depend on the Closed Type selected. Some Closed Types don't have any associated Closed Reasons, in which case you can just leave this blank.
    • Add any comments you have as needed.
    • Indicate whether to consider the member for next year.
  • Once you click Next, you'll be taken to a screen where you can confirm you are ready to completely close the enrollment, as you will no longer be able to edit the record once you do so. Confirming and clicking Next will automatically move the Registration Stage to Exited. The Closed Registration Details section on the Volunteer Registration will show all the information you just entered, including the Registration Stage at the time of closing.


Helpful Tools

In the actions panel, you can use the following buttons to facilitate certain steps during the enrollment process. See here for more about the information needed to make these buttons available, and to move the record to the next stage.

  • Create Acknowledgement Form: Prefills an acknowledgement form for you to sign. Once you sign, it will save as a Secure Document.
  • Run NSOPW: Opens the NSOPW website in a new tab and searches member's name.
  • Enroll in My AmeriCorps: This button will only display on records for AmeriCorps members. It opens the eGrants website in a new tab and prefills the enrollment form with details from the Volunteer Registration.
  • Send Release and Confidentiality: Emails the member a prefilled Release and Confidentiality form to sign. Once they sign, it will save as a Secure Document.
  • Send Parent/Guardian Consent Form: Sends a prefilled consent form to the Parent/Guardian Email listed on the member's Person record. Once they sign, it will save as a Secure Document.
  • Send Enrollment Packet: Emails the member a prefilled contract, code of conduct, and any applicable addenda to sign. If they are a minor, their parent/guardian will also have to sign their part. Once they submit, you will receive an email prompting you to sign your part. It will then save as a Secure Document.
  • Send AmeriCorps Eligibility: Emails a prefilled AmeriCorps Eligibility form to the member for them to sign. Once they sign, you will receive an email prompting you to sign your part (if they are a minor, their parent/guardian will be prompted to sign before you). It will then save as a Secure Document.
  • Send File Upload Request: If you need to collect additional documents from the member, such as their ID, you can click this button to send them a link to a form where they can upload it securely.
  • Quick Copy Details: For external forms that we cannot prefill, you can quickly copy key details from the record such as name, phone number, birthday, and social security number without worrying if you missed a character.
