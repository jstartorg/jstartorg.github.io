#process #staffing #onboarding

Generally consultants should be treated like employees, given their own hardware, user account, and licenses with the expectation they will use them when doing Jumpstart work. Since there are a variety of types of consultants, there can be differences in specifics, but the starting place for the conversation is here and it can be tweaked when there is a compelling case1 to do so. Also they MUST take the WISP course and signify they agree to its constraints.

Initiation

It is ideal for a department to reach out to OpsTech before engaging an external consultant, so we can make sure the appropriate tools will be ready to go right away, and any potential questions or kinks can be worked out ahead of time. This rarely happens. In any case, when the department engages a consultant they should complete a Personnel Form as they would for any new employee.

Hardware

Since Jumpstart computers have full disk encryption, antivirus software, firewalls, remote support, and a standard array of applications pre-installed it is important that consultants working on Jumpstart data use a Jumpstart provided computer. If they will not be dealing with sensitive or PII then there can be greater flexibility in what hardware they use, but there should be a verification by OpsTech that data access is not a concern.

Software and Licensing

Every consultant should have access to our standard software, most especially if they will be interacting with any of our systems. The place to start is with an Active Directory account, set up in the External Users OU. From there they can be added to any relevant groups. By default they will get an Office 365 E1 license, which will give them an email address, allow them to use Teams, and allow them to login to any system that supports SSO.

Beyond those basics they may need other licenses, which may entail a cost and should be accounted for by the hiring department1. Most of our licenses are transferrable and do not become tied to a single user, so very likely they will just need to be temporarily assigned a spare license for the duration of their working relationship with Jumpstart. Salesforce is a good example of a license with significant cost, but that we always have a spare of and can re-assigned easily. Some of the licenses take a little more investigation - someone may want to have the desktop versions of the Office suite which requires an Office 365 E5 license, but given the cost of those it should be clarified why they need the desktop apps as part of their consultancy, and why they don't already have access to it as a professional consultant. On the other hand if they are closer to a temp worker filling in, it may be very reasonable that they don't have Office 365 already.

Security

The general security principles should be followed, making sure to understand exactly what they need and how long they need it before granting access.

Wants versus Needs

An all too common issue in working with consultants can be that they or their contacts can overestimate need and underestimate effort. Someone may ask for a login to Salesforce when all they need is the export of one report. Someone may ask for a quick email blast to alumni but want to include person-specific information that isn't even stored in the system. It is critical to ask questions, check for understanding, and be sure the consultant themselves has a good understanding of what they are asking for, and what timeline they have in mind. A consultant works on just their project, and it is their only (relevant) priority - they will most often demand as much attention as we can give as a natural extension of their more limited focus. Always clarify the trade-offs with management when one project is prioritized above another. And remember, consultants don't have to live in the houses they build, we do.
