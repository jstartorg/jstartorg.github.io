- Document technical and functional use cases and preliminary requirements before engaging a contractor or a vendor.

- Find an approved partner from the AppExchange, SFDO website, or from referrals from other organizations.

- Engage business owners, IT, and procurement in the development of RFPs to ensure that what you are trying to accomplish is clearly communicated. Make sure that functional, technical, and business-focused questions are covered.

- Make sure that vendors competing for contracts from your organization are asked the same questions during the vetting process to make sure that competition is fair.

- Expect vendors to ask questions and to have a process for disseminating your responses to all vendors. Some will try to disrupt that process as a sales tactic; you do not need to accommodate requests outside your established process.

- Have potential vendors or consulting partners provide you with references.

- Ask other organizations about potential contractors and vendors. This is an established practice in higher education that organizations (non-profits, etc.,) can do as well.

- Use this partner assessment tool to help you evaluate vendors.

- Confirm who will be working with your team from vendors, how long they will be on the project if any others will be joining or replacing them during the engagement, and how the contractor manages that process.

- Implement strong project management practices on both your team and with the vendor.

- Communicate decisions and actions clearly and consistently with your contractor or vendor during the engagement.

- Plan and budget for documentation and other post-go-live support to position your organization for success after the vendor engagement is over.

- Determine what kind of compensation model makes sense before the engagement ex: (1) pay on an hourly basis with a  not to exceed $ clause or (2) structure the project to be a  fixed-cost, all-in regardless of the number of hours.

- Evaluate your vendor engagements after they wrap up with a structured process.

- Share your experience with vendors & contractors with other organizations!
