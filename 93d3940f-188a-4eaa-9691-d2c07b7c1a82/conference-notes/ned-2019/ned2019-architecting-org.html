Building a Better House: (Re)Architecting an Existing Org

Business Operating Models

  • Coordination
  • Unification: single org, shared data, look at customer the same way
  • Diversification
  • Replication

Systems/Processes Involved

  • Describe the business
  • · Describe business process
  • Define entities
  • Define relationship
  • What is critical and important to the business?
  • Purpose is data design

Multiple Lines of Business

  • Complexity of multiple lines of business: business processes are usually mostly the same
  • Security model
  • Mature apex design
  • Complex data models

Data Life Cycle

  • Source of truth for each entity
  • Where do updates and inserts originate?
  • Who owns data and data stewardship?
  • Reporting and metrics
  • Is business process initiated/influenced by SF?

Salesforce Architecture Considerations

  • OLTP: online transaction process. SF updates itself
  • Multi-tenancy model: logically isolated customer orgs sharing resources
  • Data relationships: predefined relationships between objects
  • Not a data warehouse: not everything should be stored in SF!

Standard vs Custom Objects

  • Don’t recreate standard objects

Limits of an Instance

  • Governor
  • Platform
  • API
  • Custom Code
    • Stick to process builder and flow vs workflow rules
    • Stay as declarative as possible
  • Scalability thresholds

Object Classification Model

Core Layer

  • Managed the most
  • Large data volumes
  • Used across all areas of business
  • Accounts, contacts, opps, etc

Managed Layer

  • Important but not mission critical
  • More stabilized
  • Medium to large data volumes

Delegated Administration Layer

  • One-offs
  • Helps support major process
  • Hardly any major workflows
  • Little volume
  • Mainly stores data

Data Standards

  • What is on and off the platform
  • Use picklists as much as possible
  • Create naming conventions
  • Define record types and why
  • Define how objects relate
  • Specify what objects and fields are part of integration in case API names change

Regulations, Compliance, and Security

  • Personal identifiable information
  • Encryption
  • Confidential information
  • IP restrictions

Security and Sharing Model

Start with profiles and permissions and go up the chain (see chart in deck)

Reporting & Analytics

  • What is operational (what is happening right now, transactional) vs what is analytical (over time, trending, should happen out of data warehouse, not SF)
  • Use out-of-the-box as much as possible
  • Consider integrated reporting so people don’t leave the platform

Archive & Retention Policy

  • Have backup tools and policies
  • Keep data in SF as small as possible bc costly, when possible move it elsewhere
  • Do a nightly backup, feed your data warehouse with a backup
  • Have backups for recovery, but don’t use them for integrations or reporting

Create Objects and Relationships

Always do this last

Effective Change Management

  • Plan strategy for change, find a change sponsor
  • Gather change request ideas, define change scope
  • Prioritize, configure, and test
  • Educate and train end users, deploy, gather and respond to feedback

Customer 360

Should have a holistic view of customer

Three Takeaways

  • People are at the heart of the restructure
  • Process: difference between spring cleaning and rearchitecting is the driver of change
  • Technology: SF is always used in combination with other systems
