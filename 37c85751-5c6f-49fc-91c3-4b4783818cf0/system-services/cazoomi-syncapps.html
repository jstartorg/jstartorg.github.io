Description

#paid #communication #integration

sync platform for integrating Salesforce and Constant Contact. We use it to sync Salesforce Contacts and Leads to Constant Contact. It also syncs back into Salesforce updates on unsubscribe actions (checking the “HasOptedOutOfEmail” box on the record) as well as actions taken on an email send such as Opens and Clicks. Note: The value of the event data being pushed back into Salesforce is worth examining as it consumes storage but little is likely actually done with that data.

All “Active” Salesforce Campaigns with the Record Type “Mailing List” are synced as Lists in Constant Contact. The Contacts are also filtered for any that have already opted out of recieving emails.

Leads

In order to facilitate large scale communication with Corps Members, both active and alumni, Leads from Salesforce are being synced into Constant Contact. Policies about emailing Corps Members have a greater number of limitations and severity of consequence, so the details of the sync must make sure to capture the data needed to comply clearly.

In order to make sure only appropriate emails are sent to individuals the “Do Not Solicit for Money” and “Do Not Contact about Advocacy” fields are assigned the value found in the “Is Active” field on the Lead record - if they are actively serving, they cannot be contacted about money or political advocacy.

Another aspect of syncing Leads is that anyone who also has a normal Contact record, as most of them will eventually have, will not be synced twice to Constant Contact. Instead the email address is treated as the unique identifier and all syncs regardless of source use it. There is the possibility that one person may have more than one email on file with Jumpstart and thus may recieve multiple emails, but they will go to different inboxes and if they elect to unsubscribe one of the accounts the other will be left unchanged.

Salesforce Campaign Management

General Campaign Notes
Something to note is that when the sync writes back to Salesforce it will sometimes create Salesforce Campaigns to match Lists created first in Constant Contact. When it does that it will set a hidden custom field on the Campaign called “Cazoomi_campaign_id__c” and not include it in the sync from Salesforce to Constant Contact, so it should be used only for reference not contact management in Salesforce.
