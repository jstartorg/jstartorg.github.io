Selected Page User Interface

Homepage

Homepage
-The homepage is the first impression visitors may have of the organization.
-It should be designed to clearly communicate Jumpstart's mission, vision, and values.
-It shoud inspire visitors to take action and support its cause.
-More effectively highlight top 3 categories Join Us, Donors, and Partnerships

Headline
-The headline should be keyword-optimized, clear and concise, and summarizes what Jumpstart does and why it matters.

  • Current headline, "COVID-19 Information & Updates"

MIssion Statement
-Explains who Jumpstart is, why you do, and why you do it.
-Consider placing it prominently on homepage above the fold below the hero image.

Programs and Impact
-Homepage should highlight main programs and services that Jumpstart offers, the outcomes and impact they have acheived.
-Use images, statistics, testimonials to show how Jumpstart helps children develop essential skills.

Calls-to-Action
-When dealing with multiple CTAs on a page, it's important to maintain clarity, guide user actions, and avoid overwhelming the visitors.
-Establish a clear visual hierarchy among the CTAs, emphasizing the most important action.
-Use visual cues like size, color, or placement to differentiate the primary CTA from secondary ones.
-Reserve the most prominent position, such as the top right corner, for the primary CTA.
-Start with the primary CTA that aligns with the primary goal of the page.
-Arrange the secondary CTAs in a logical order, considering the priority and user expectations.
-The calls to action in the top navigation, Join the Corps and Donate disappear as you scroll down the page.
-On the mobile site, they aren't present.
-Consider using sticky or fixed positioning for CTAs to keep them easily accessible as users scroll on mobile.

Accessibility
-For accessiblity, we recoomend impementing an app such as Accessibe.
-Apps like this provide easy options to adjust the presentation of the website and design to best meet individual needs.

Website Theme

Main Menu

Importance and Priority
-Arrange the menu items based on their importance and priority. Place the most critical or frequently accessed sections towards the left-hand side of the menu.

Clear and Concise Navigation
-The main menu should have a clear and easy-to-understand navigation structure.
-Use concise and descriptive labels for each menu item.
-Prioritize the most important and relevant sections of the website.

Highlight Important Pages
-Identify key pages or sections that are crucial for Jumpstart's goals and highlight them in the main menu.
-Make these high-priority items easily accessible and visually distinguishable within the menu.

Drop Down Menu
-Position important pages or menu items higher in the drop-down menu hierarchy. Typically, the most crucial or frequently accessed pages should be placed closer to the parent menu item or at higher levels of the drop-down menu. This ensures that important content is easily accessible and visible to users.
-Use visual cues to draw attention to important pages within the drop-down menu. This can be done through font styling, color differentiation, or using icons or badges to signify the significance of specific menu items. Visual emphasis helps users quickly identify and understand the importance of certain pages or sections.

Website Theme
-Based on the menu recommendations, the decision on whether to change Jumpstart's current website theme depends on the theme's flexibility in terms of menu customization, navigation structure, highlighting important pages, and organizing drop-down menus.

Donations

Call to Action
Make the CTA more prominent in the top navigation using contrasting color and large font size.
The Donate page should be easy to find at all times.

Include a Compelling "Why"
Explain why Jumpstart needs the donations and how they will make a difference for the children and the communities they serve.

Simplify the Donation Process
Keep the donation process simple and streamlined with minimal steps.
Avoid asking for unnecessary or excessive personal information, as it may discourage potential donors.
We recommend reviewing the information in the exisitng form and minimizing the required fields to the essentials.

Transparency
Provide clear and concise information about how donations will be used and the impact they will have.
We recommend implementing an FAQ or questions section to include the Questions, Privacy Policy, Disclosures, etc. in a more concise format.

Trust
Include testimonial and/or success stories, or statistics to build trust and demonstrate credibility.

Social Proof
Incorporate social proof elements such as the numer of donors or testimonials from satisfied donors.

Website Theme
-Consider changing the website theme if the current theme restricts customization options for the prominent placement of the donation call to action (CTA) in the top navigation.
-Evaluate the theme's ability to effectively convey a compelling "Why" for donations and the impact they make. If the current theme lacks suitable sections or formatting options, a theme change may be beneficial.
-Assess if the current theme supports a streamlined and user-friendly donation process. If the donation process is complex or asks for excessive personal information, consider a theme change to provide a simpler experience.
