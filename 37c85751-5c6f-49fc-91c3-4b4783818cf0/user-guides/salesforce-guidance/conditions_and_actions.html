Stage Triggers for Volunteer Registrations

Triggers in general evaluate the Corps Member’s information, the requirements of their commission or state, and the documentation provided. Relevant dates and confirmations can be manually entered, though in each case there are several tools to help make the process more streamlined (the form sending buttons, for instance). At a high level, the stage criteria are

  • Applying: Application submitted by CM
  • Enrolling: Criminal History Check (CHC) Authorization Form Date and an Accepted Offer Date
  • Serving: All required CHC component dates, a Service Start and Exit Date, and a Contract Signed Date
  • Suspended: Instructions on suspending members can be found here. Any button listed below that shows when the Registration Stage is Serving will show when Registration Stage is Suspended as well.
  • Exiting: Service Exit Date is today or before
  • Exited: Site staff have completed the necessary exit steps

Applying

This is the first and default stage. When an application is submitted by anyone, first-timer or returner, they enter the Applying stage. This stage has its own sub-process stages called Application Process Stages.

No Action Yet

This is the default stage all applications come in at. It is used to filter some list views to make sure program staff can see the latest applications that come in. It is similar to emails being marked Unread.

Scheduling

Corps Members (CMs) enter the Scheduling stage if program staff use the Schedule Interview widget on the Volunteer Registration page. It is not essential, and if instead staff want to schedule an interview on their own, they can fill in the “Interview: Scheduled Time” field themselves.

Interviewing

CMs will be in the interviewing stage after the “Interview: Scheduled Time” field is filled in, either manually or as a response to the choice they make from the Calendly invite they get from the Schedule Interview widget.

Offering

Temporary stage that most CMs will not enter but will potentially be used if program staff need to track that a CM has been interviewed but is not ready to start submitting documents.

Accepted Offer

Final Application Process Stage for potential CMs who will be moving into service. The trigger for this stage has multiple requirements:

  • Service Dates (both Start and Exit) must be filled in
  • Accepted Offer Date must be filled in
    • Once Service Dates are entered, the “Send Offer Form” button will be available. CMs who complete this form electronically will have their Accepted Offer Date automatically filled in

Closed Application

If an applicant is not a good fit, or must drop out because of time constraints, their application gets closed out with the “Close Application” tool, which allows for staff to pick a main reason, provide notes for more specificity, and also allows indicating that an applicant should be considered in the next year. This tool can be used at any point in the Application Process to close an application and remove the applicant from staff views. The data is not deleted but is archived for potential future reference.

Enrolling

The enrolling stage is focused on beginning the Criminal History Check process, completing the CM Enrollment packet (Contract, Code of Conduct), and setting up their financial details if applicable. All the requirements for fulfilling the “Accepted Offer” Application Process stage must be met, and additionally:

  • Either "History Check: Authorization Form Signed" field is filled in
    • Using the "Send Authorization Form" button will automatically fill in the History Check: Authorization Form Signed date when the form is submitted by the CM
  • Or the CM is marked as exempt from the CHC process
  • Or the CM is serving in a state where Jumpstart does not manage the Criminal History Check process

CHC Status

The Status field next to the CHC date input fields indicates how far along they are in the CHC process. The definitions of each status are:

  • Not Cleared: Corps Member cannot begin earning hours of any type because one or more of the following has not been completed: NSOPW check, criminal history check authorization form, all other required component initiations.
  • Initiated: (for Corps Members who can begin their association with Jumpstart before full clearance) NSOPW has been cleared, and other required checks have been initiated. These checks must still be cleared prior to any service hours or access to children.
  • Needs Acknowledgement: Corps Member has received full clearance for all required criminal history checks and the Acknowledgement Form may now be created and completed to indicate the checks have been conducted and considered.
  • Cleared for Training and Service: Corps Member has fully cleared the NSOPW, states of service and of residence checks, and the FBI fingerprint check, and the Acknowledgement Form has been printed or saved as a pdf.

Special Note – Jumpstart User Account Creation

When a CM reaches Enrolling or Serving, the system will evaluate the record every time it is updated to see if a Jumpstart user account needs to be generated. Their Jumpstart account allows them to access MyJstart, Microsoft Teams, a site YouTube channel, and other resources as relevant. The criteria are

  • They don't already have a Jumpstart account (CM accounts will be reactivated when they serve subsequent years without an email notice or password reset required)
  • Their Email is not @mailinator.com (so you can create test records easily without junking up your user list)
  • Either
    • the Registration Stage is Serving
    • Or
      • Registration Stage is Enrolling
      • And all Criminal History Checks are complete (or initiated, where initiation is tracked)

Serving

The focus of the serving stage is managing the CM's team involvement, financial details, and training. There are several different ways for a CM to enter serving based on the state they are serving in and CHC requirements:

  • Either
    • The Contract Signed Date is filled in
    • And either
      • Clearance is required for member to begin service
      • And CHC Status is Cleared for Training and Service
    • Or
      • Clearance is not required for member to begin service
      • And CHC Status is Initiated
  • Or
    • Jumpstart does not manage the CHC process in the Service State (e.g. AR or MS)
    • And Application Process Stage is “Accepted Offer”

Suspended

Instructions on suspending members can be found here. Any button listed below that shows when the Registration Stage is Serving will show when Registration Stage is Suspended as well.

Exiting

Exiting a CM can involve several steps such as completing a performance review or certifying their final hours in MyAmeriCorps. The trigger for this stage is

  • Registration Stage is Enrolling, Serving, or Suspended
  • Service Exit Date is less than or equal to today’s date

Exited

After completing the necessary forms and actions in the Exiting stage, a CM record is finally marked as Exited. The trigger for this stage is manual - site staff must complete the Exit Member process using the button in the actions panel.

Backdating Forms

The signature date(s) on these forms will generally default to the current date. The date(s) can always be edited when signing the form in case it needs to be backdated, but if you want to make it a bit easier on the signer(s) by prefilling the signature date(s) with a specific backdate, switch the Backdating Form? toggle at the bottom of the Actions Panel to Yes, enter the signature date as the Backdate Value, and click Next (when you set the Backdate Value, the form buttons will disappear to prevent you from clicking the button until the date has been applied, so you may have to scroll up a bit to click Next). Then, the buttons will reload and you'll see a confirmation message that the backdate has been set, so when you click one of the form buttons it will use that backdate. You can always refresh the page to clear or change the backdate.

Note that there are certain buttons where custom backdating doesn't apply, and these buttons will disappear when you set a custom backdate. This includes forms that don't have a signature date, and forms with signature dates that should always match a specific field on the Volunteer Registration (such as the enrollment package, which is always backdated to the Service Start Date, or the application, which is always backdated to the Application Date).

In general, prefilling the backdate is helpful but not required. However, the one situation where you'll need to do so is if the Corps Member is not currently a minor but was a minor on the backdate, and the form you're using has sections related to minors. When you prefill the backdate and send the form, the system will know the person was a minor as of the backdate, and the sections of the form related to minors will display properly.
